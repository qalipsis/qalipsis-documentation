= QALIPSIS GUI

== Overview

The QALIPSIS GUI allows you to create, configure, run, monitor and view results of test campaigns from your browser.
You can configure campaigns and run them immediately, at a future time, or on a recurring schedule.

* You can view in-process campaign results in real time.
* You can view finalized reports of a previous campaign's data on static reports and on an interactive time-series chart.   

The following topics are discussed in this section:

* <<Logging in to QALIPSIS>>
* <<Navigating QALIPSIS>>
* <<Working with series data and filters>>
* <<Configuring and running a campaign>>
* <<Displaying campaign data>>

== Logging in to QALIPSIS

image::GUI\Tenant-select-Login.jpg[float="right"]

. Navigate to the QALIPSIS Welcome page according to the installation details provided by your administrator.
+
QALIPSIS Cloud is available at https://app.qalipsis.io.
. Enter your Username and Password.
+
If your user profile is eligible for several organizations, select the tenant to use from the drop down box.
+
. Click btn:[Submit] to log into QALIPSIS.

*Notes*::

* If a Tenant selection message is not displayed, you will be logged in to QALIPSIS with the tenant you were using in your most recent login.
* You can can select a different tenant at any time that you are logged into QALIPSIS, using the bottom-left panel.

When you log into QALIPSIS, the _Home_ page of the GUI and a left sidebar are displayed.

* Near the top of the sidebar, a menu is displayed. The contents of the menu depends on your profile permissions.

image::GUI\tenant-sidebars-resized.jpg[float="right"]
+
From the displayed menu you have several options.

** <<Access campaign information and functions>>
** <<Access Series information and functions>>
** <<Access administrative functions>>
+
* Near the bottom of the sidebar, your user name is displayed with your current tenant.

image::GUI\tenant-modal-window-resized.jpg[align="center"]

From the _Tenant Modal_ you have a few options.

** <<Change to a different tenant>>
** <<View/Update Your profile>>

Note:: The left sidebar remains displayed throughout your session.  

=== Change to a different tenant
. Click on your name near the bottom of the sidebar to open the _Tenant Modal_ window. 
. Select an available tenant to use for the current session.


=== View/Update Your profile
. Click on your name at the bottom of the left sidebar to open the  _Tenant Modal_ window.
. Click on the `settings` icon.
. Click on `My profile` to open the _My Profile_ page.
. Make changes to your profile and click on `Save changes`.

image::GUI\ggllcprofile.jpg[align="center"]

=== Change your password
. Click on your name at the bottom of the left sidebar to open the  _Tenant Modal_ window.
. Click on the `settings` icon.
. Click on `My profile` to open the _My Profile_ page.
. Click on `Change password`.
. Enter your new password in the `New Password` and `Comfirm Password` fields.
. Click on `Save changes` to save your new password. 

=== Logout 
. Click on your name at the bottom of the left sidebar to open the _Tenant Modal_ window.
. Click on the `settings` icon.
. Click the btn:[Logout] button.
You will be logged out and returned to the _Welcome_ screen.

== Working with series data and filters
Series are defined events and meters that are logged while a campaign is in progress.

* The logged events and meters can be graphically displayed 
* You can create a series if your profile has write permissions. 
* You can update a series if one of the following is true.
** You are the owner
** The owner has shared it in write mode and you have write permissions.

=== Creating a series
From the Tenant sidebar:

. Click on `Series` to open the _Series_ page.
+
The _Series_  page displays a table listing currently defined series.
+
image::GUI\seriespage.jpg[align="center"]
+
. Click the btn:[+New series] button near the top right corner of the page.
+
The _New series_ modal window opens. 
. Enter the required data into the appropriate fields.
+
Note that only numeric fields can be used with an operation. The the other types, only the count of provided values can be configured.
  
. Click the btn:[Create] button.
+
The following text is displayed in the modal window: '[Series Name] has just been created!'. 
. Close the  _New series_ modal window to return to the Series page.  
+
The newly created series is displayed in the table.

=== Updating a series
. Click on *Series* in the left sidebar menu.
+
The _Series_ page opens and displays a table listing currently defined series.
. Click on the name of the series you want to update. 
+
The _Update series_ modal window opens. 
. Enter the required data into the appropriate fields.
. Click the btn:[Update] button.
. Close the _Update series_ modal window to return to the Series page.  

== Configuring and running a campaign

. Click the btn:[+Campaign] button near the top right of the `Campaign` page.
+
The New Campaign window will open displaying the campaign name, a settings icon, a search field, and a table listing scenarios that are available to run in the campaign.

image::GUI\new-campaign.jpg[align="center"]

. Optionally, rename the campaign by hovering over the `New Campaign` title and selecting the pen icon to edit the name.
  
. Select the checkbox to the left of each scenario that you want to include in your campaign. 
+
Each scenario has a default configuration.  
+
. To configure a scenario to better suit your specific requirements, click the btn:[Configure] button to the right of the scenario. 

. Update the fields on the _Scenario Configuration_ window.
. Click  *Apply* and close the window. 
+
Scenario changes are saved for the current campaign and do not affect the scenario's default settings.

. Click the btn:[Run immediately] button to start the campaign. 
+
Campaign data is displayed in real-time in the report window and on the time-series chart.

** Minion count is displayed by default.
** Select one or more series cards to display the generated data for that series.

image::GUI\Checkout-stresstest campaign details.jpg[align="center"]


== Displaying campaign data
. From the left sidebar:
. Click on *Campaign* in the left sidebar.
+
A table listing all existing and planned campaigns is displayed.

* Click on any column title to sort the table by ascending/descending order.
* Search for a specific campaign by clicking on the magnifying glass icon near the top right of the screen and entering the name (or partial name) of the campaign.

. Click on the name of a campaign to display the _Campaign details_ page.
+
* A report box is displayed at the top of the screen.
+
The top line in the report box displays information for the  whole campaign; the following lines display information for each scenario within the campaign.

** Click the `View more` tooltip on the right side of the report box to expand the report box to display all scenarios in the campaign.
** Click the `More details` tooltip to display the info for the total campaign in a dropdown box.
** Click on the `Campiagn Summary` dropdown filter to select the scenario information to display in the report box.

* Below the report box, a row of filter cards is displayed with the individual events and meters that are logged for the campaign. 
+
Refer to link:05_steps.adoc[`Step Specifications`] or <<Creating a series>> for guidance creating filtering steps. 
+
Each card includes the Name, Type, and display color.

* Below the row of filter cards, a time-series chart is displayed with the x-axis displaying the elapsed time  and the y-axis displaying series data.

Select one or more series cards to display the generated data for that series.

image::GUI\Checkout-stresstest campaign details.jpg[align="center"]
