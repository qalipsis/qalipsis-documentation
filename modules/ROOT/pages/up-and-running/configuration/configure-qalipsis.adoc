= Configuring QALIPSIS [.badge.oss]## ##[.badge.commercial]## ##

QALIPSIS is extremely powerful and configurable.

To make it easy and fast to use, it comes with a default set of configuration, that might not exactly meet your requirements.

There are really many things you can configure, but do not be afraid.

We will get through this section and discover how easy it is to customize the execution of QALIPSIS.

[NOTE]
====
Be sure you have read and understood xref:up-and-running/up-and-running.adoc[Installing QALIPSIS] before working through this section.
====

Here is a small overview of the different parts composing this section:

- xref:up-and-running/configuration/provide-configuration.adoc[Provide configuration] explains how to specify the configuration to QALIPSIS.
- xref:up-and-running/configuration/configure-data-storage.adoc[Configure data storage] explains how to configure QALIPSIS to store the data.
- xref:up-and-running/configuration/monitoring-test-campaigns.adoc[Monitoring test campaigns] will guide you through the steps required to store and use  analytics data generated the tests.
- xref:up-and-running/configuration/reporting.adoc[Reporting] details how to configure the reporting in QALIPSIS.
- xref:up-and-running/configuration/logging.adoc[Logging] will guide you through the steps required to configure logging in QALIPSIS for more technical insights.
- xref:up-and-running/advanced-configuration/configuration-and-dependency-injection.adoc[Configuration and dependency injection] is rather for advanced user, and explains how to use the principles of injection of configuration and dependency (aka IoC) in your scenarios.
- xref:up-and-running/advanced-configuration/factory-executors.adoc[Factory executors] is another advanced topic that explains how to configure and use factory executors in your scenarios to optimize the performances for your test cases.
